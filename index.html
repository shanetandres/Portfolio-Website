Beehive Audio ML Classification

Group Project

At my university I am one of the leaders of WaggleNet, a student-run research group focused on using sensors and analytics to monitor the health of beehives. In the group, I lead the audio team. Over the past year, I have had the opportunity to deploy a recording device I created at the university's bee lab. At the end of the Fall 2024 semester, I was able to record 6 days worth of data from a hive. Using the data, I trained a classification model to distinguish between disturbed and undisturbed hives. I used an FFT to extract features from audio clips, and experimented with different deep net architectures. Using logistic regression, I was able to achieve 90% validation accuracy on our dataset. Moving forward, I plan to explore more sophisticated methods for feature extraction that encode time dependencies in the data. Future work will also make use of ML architecture that takes advantage of these dependencies, such as CNNs and RNNs.

FPGA Deep Net Implementation

Independent School Project, 2024

During the Fall 2024 semester, I took a class on FPGA design at my university. For the final project, I implemented the forward pass of an MLP deep net in hardware with SystemVerilog. I trained a model on the MNIST digit detection dataset using PyTorch, and loaded the weights onto the hardware. Using a serial connection, the FPGA was able to receive and classify input images in live time. The model performed with 97% validation accuracy.
